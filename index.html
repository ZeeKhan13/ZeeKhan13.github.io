Background

About Me

I am a researcher and engineer in therapeutic ultrasound, with many years of experience spanning academia and industry. My work integrates transducer development, acoustic and biothermal modeling, and translational validation to advance safe, effective non-invasive clinical applications.

After completing my PhD at Imperial College London, I held postdoctoral positions at UCSF and Stanford University, focusing on deployable ultrasound applicators for cancer therapy and focused ultrasound neuromodulation for multiple sclerosis.

I currently serve as Principal Ultrasound Engineer at OpenWater Health Inc., where I lead the development of low-frequency phased-array therapeutic ultrasound platforms and drive FDA regulatory development including 510(k) contributions.

I am open to research collaborations, consulting engagements, and academic and industry partnerships in therapeutic ultrasound and bioengineering.

Research Areas

Active

Wearable Modular Therapeutic Ultrasound Platform for Neuromodulation

End-to-end development of a wearable, electronically-steerable low-frequency phased-array platform for transcranial focused ultrasound neuromodulation — spanning system architecture, transducer design, beamforming, acoustic and biothermal modeling, bench and in vitro validation, V&V frameworks, contract manufacturer collaboration, and 510(k) regulatory readiness toward clinical translation.

Wearable LIFUPhased ArraysClinical Translation510(k)V&V
Active

Deployable Catheter-Based Ultrasound Applicators

Developed minimally invasive endoluminal and laparoscopic catheter-based phased-array applicators with expandable reflector balloons for MR-guided tumor ablation, hyperthermia, and pancreatic cancer therapy at UCSF (NIH R01 & R21).

Completed · Stanford

Focused Ultrasound for Multiple Sclerosis

Led interdisciplinary project on FUS neuromodulation for MS in preclinical models at Stanford. Developed spinal cord targeting strategies, acoustic and biothermal modeling pipelines, and experimental–computational workflows linking simulations with in vivo biological outcomes.

FUSMS / Spinal CordIn vivoTreatment Planning
Completed · Imperial

Dual-Mode Ultrasound Imaging & Therapy with Random Phased Arrays

PhD research at Imperial College London. Designed an integrated imaging-therapy platform using a therapeutic random phased array with Verasonics Vantage, enabling 3D synthetic aperture imaging and transcostal targeted ablation with adaptive beamforming.

R&D Consulting — Neuromodulation to Histotripsy

R&D consulting for multiple medical device companies spanning the full therapeutic ultrasound spectrum — from focused ultrasound neuromodulation and custom transducer design to extracorporeal HIFU and histotripsy — including simulation-driven optimization within patient-specific anatomical models and preclinical system deployment.

Transducer DesignNeuromodulationHistotripsyPatient-specific Models

Ultrasound Hyperthermia Treatment Planning

Designed and validated treatment planning frameworks for ultrasound hyperthermia using ~1–3 MHz clinical systems. Demonstrated volumetric hyperthermia feasibility in head & neck cancer patient-specific models using spherical random phased array transducers.
